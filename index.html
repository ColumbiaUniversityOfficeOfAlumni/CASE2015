Illuminate: Celebrating the Columbia Campaign and Our Future

December 31, 2013 marked the conclusion of the historic $6.1 billion Columbia Campaign. To celebrate this record-breaking ten-year effort, the alumni and development marketing and communications team launched a comprehensive, multi-phase end of Campaign communications strategy: Illuminate. Echoing Columbia’s motto, In lumine Tuo videbimus lumen ("In Thy light we see light"), Illuminate aimed to appropriately thank over 205,000 donors and volunteers, celebrate our collective success, and pivot toward Columbia’s future.

The effort was inclusive across all levels and affiliations — from in person (a high-end stewardship dinner held at New York's historic Morgan Library) to online (a social media-driven day of thanks for the entire Columbia community). To highlight the human dimension and the impact of giving, we created an end of Campaign website — the Illuminate site — that featured 33 donor impact stories, an interactive Campaign data visualization, over 50 future priorities from Columbia’s schools and programs, and videos featuring a thank you from President Bollinger, an introduction to our new campus in Manhattanville, and Campaign highlights.

Objectives

Illuminate sought to celebrate the Campaign's success, highlight the University's momentum, and show the direction we are now more poised than ever to head. In addition, we wanted to give alumni and donors the opportunity to engage with and share a variety of content about Columbia and the Campaign. The goal was not merely to communicate what the Campaign did, but what an institution like Columbia can do and what donors giving through Columbia helped support.

Results

Social Media

On April 3, the Columbia Thank You Day message reached over 500,000 Facebook users and 320,000 unique followers on Twitter. Additionally, 112 social ambassadors – alumni who signed up to share Columbia content using their own social networks – drove a total reach of 104,419, with Facebook as the primary channel.

Alumni and Donor Outreach

300,000+ donors and alumni received a message of gratitude from President Bollinger via an e-mail blast and printed letters. Additionally, Columbia Magazine — which gets mailed to all 313,000 Columbia alumni — featured a Campaign story and two page "Illuminating our Future" advertorial in its Spring 2014 edition.

Stewardship Materials

2,250 donors received specialized Campaign stewardship materials. Sarah Sze, a celebrated artist on Columbia’s faculty, created an original work for the most generous donors to The Columbia Campaign, and artist and author Elisha Cooper created personalized watercolors of Butler Library and the iconic Alma Mater statue.

Campaign Celebration Dinner

Nearly 200 Campaign donors and leaders attended a dinner with President Bollinger at New York’s historic Morgan Library to celebrate The Columbia Campaign and the future it will help make possible.

Press

Articles about The Columbia Campaign were published by the Associated Press, Wall Street Journal, Bloomberg, Chronicle of Higher Education, and Inside Higher Education. It was also highlighted in Columbia publications including Columbia College Today, The Record, and the Columbia Alumni Association (CAA) newsletter, which is e-mailed to alumni worldwide.

Thanking One Another

Staff in the Office of Alumni and Development exchanged over 2,000 handwritten thank you notes. On Columbia Thank You Day, a lunch was held in the Columbia Alumni Center, where colleagues shared messages of gratitude on large "thank you boards."

Stewardship

Messaging

The Illuminate message echoes the University’s motto In lumine Tuo videbimus lumen ("In Thy light we see light"). It shines light on future plans, celebrates Columbia’s current achievements, and provided a compelling, cohesive theme for our communications and design, including the data visualization.

Thank You Day

Columbia Thank You Day was an intensive, month-long effort leading up to April 3, 2014 to reach out through social media to celebrate the more than 200,000 donors and volunteers who made The Columbia Campaign a success. It encouraged alumni, donors, and friends to share messages of gratitude as the University enters a new chapter.

Illuminate Website

The Illuminate site was created to provide a Campaign readout, show messages of gratitude and impact, and give a preview of where Columbia is headed. It is a responsive-designed website, with fluid navigation that allows easy access to a variety of multimedia content related to the end of The Columbia Campaign. It features an interactive data visualization, Campaign highlights, 33 donor impact stories, a donor roll, videos, messages from prominent Columbia alumni, infographics, and over 50 future priorities from Columbia’s schools and programs.

Data Visualization

In partnership with Periscopic, a Portland-based data visualization firm, we created an online data visualization to help donors and alumni explore the impact of the historic Columbia Campaign. It took on the challenge of translating ten years and $6.1 billion into an interactive experience that is fueled by the user’s interests and curiosity. It goes a step beyond the numbers – allowing users to explore by theme or school/program – to show stories of impact and provide a snapshot of the future the Campaign has helped make possible.

Alumni and Donor Communications

The President’s Campaign Celebration letter was sent to all donors and alumni and a Thank You Day e-mail blast was sent to all 192,000 e-mailable alumni. A Thank You video from President Bollinger was posted on the Illuminate site, and a poster featuring Campaign highlights was displayed on our main campus walkway, College Walk. The CAA also included a Thank You Day message in their newsletter and sent an e-mail blast to club leaders encouraging them to help spread the #ColumbiaThankYouDay message.

Stewardship

Top Campaign donors were invited to join President Bollinger at a Campaign Celebration Dinner at New York’s historic Morgan Library. In addition, 2,250 donors received works of art, including a print by celebrated artist and professor Sarah Sze and watercolors of Butler Library and the iconic Alma Mater statue. Donors of $100,000 or more were publicly acknowledged on a donor roll on the Illuminate website.
